Our Patient Portal Services

Patient portals are centralized, secure platforms that provide personalized, fully integrated access to information and services. They offer healthcare organizations a convenient way to streamline access to medical records, appointment scheduling, and communication with healthcare providers.

  • Consulting

    Our highly skilled consultants help medical organizations expand their patient engagement ecosystem by advising on a suitable patient portal solution and providing expert assistance with its implementation.

  • Development

    We can develop a custom patient portal or implement a platform-based solution tailored to your organization's needs, ensuring operational and data safety through modern cybersecurity principles.

  • Integration

    We follow a rigorous integration process to ensure seamless interoperability. This includes integrating patient portals with other healthcare and administrative software, such as EMR/EHR systems and healthcare CRMs.

  • Modernization

    Our team will evaluate the effectiveness of your existing portal against current needs, add new modules like telemedicine and patient education, and integrate additional solutions to enhance functionality, creating a more user-friendly and effective patient portal.

  • Compliance

    We offer comprehensive support to maintain fully operational, secure, and cost-efficient patient portals. This includes L1-L3 support, proactive performance monitoring, and on-demand upgrades and patches.

  • QA & Testing

    We help healthcare companies keep their patient portals fully operable, secure, and cost-effective by providing L1-L3 support services, performance monitoring, and on-demand upgrades and patch installation.

Key patient portal solutions capabilities

Patient portals include a range of essential features that support each step of a patient’s medical journey.

  • Access to records

    Authorized patients can access their health examination summaries, lab results, interactions with medical professionals, self-recorded symptoms, wearables, and medical device data stored in the organization’s database.

  • Communication

    Patients with an internet connection can contact their care team in real-time without going to the doctor’s office to consult about non-urgent issues or clarify healthcare-related questions.

  • Patient self-service

    Patients or their representatives can schedule appointments, online consultations, or prescription refills, contact customer support, enter their health data, and sign up for a clinical trial program.

  • Patient Education

    A patient portal can be an educational hub that promotes a healthy lifestyle, helps patients understand their conditions and treatment better, and alerts them of potential health risks.

  • Feedback aggregation

    Patients and care providers can complete forms via patient portals to evaluate the quality of the organization’s care and customer services, work conditions, and other parameters.

  • Insurance management

    With the help of payment system integration, patients can view and manage their insurance claims and payments for each medical event.
