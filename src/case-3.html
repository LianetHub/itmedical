About the Client

Our customer is a US-based provider of health information solutions for oncologists. They wanted a platform that would help doctors make evidence-based treatment decisions.

Challenge

Creating a Comprehensive Oncology Treatment Platform: Overcoming Knowledge Management and Reporting Limitations

The customer had a working prototype software platform that allowed them to collect disease reports and research from relevant sources and store them. However, it did not meet all their requirements, including knowledge base management and report generation. The client wanted a solution that would streamline patient case analysis and facilitate treatment selection through access to relevant research studies.

  1. Knowledge Base Management: The prototype lacked an efficient way to manage and organize the vast amount of collected research and disease reports. A comprehensive knowledge base was needed to make this information easily accessible and usable by medical experts.
  2. Automated Report Generation: The existing platform did not support the automatic generation of detailed reports, facilitating informed treatment decisions. This functionality saves time and ensures consistency in the information provided to oncologists.
  3. Streamlined Patient Case Analysis: The platform needed to facilitate the analysis of patient cases by integrating relevant research studies directly into the decision-making process. This would help doctors select the most appropriate treatments based on the latest evidence.
  4. Integration with Existing Systems: To be effective, the new solution had to integrate seamlessly with existing Electronic Health Records (EHR) and Laboratory Information Management Systems (LIMS), as well as with laboratory and insurance service providers. This integration ensures that all relevant patient data and research information are available in one place.
  5. User Access and Permissions: Medical experts, curators, doctors, and nurses required different levels of access. The platform needed a robust system to manage these varying levels of access and ensure that sensitive information was only available to authorized personnel.

Given these challenges, the task was to develop a comprehensive oncology treatment platform that would address these specific needs, enhance decision-making, and streamline patient care processes.

Outcome

Transforming Oncology Treatment with a Comprehensive Platform: Improved Decision-Making and Patient Care

The IT Medical team delivered a custom oncology treatment platform that streamlines patient care by enabling doctors to make accurate decisions based on the most relevant studies. This allows them to prescribe the appropriate therapy for each patient. The implementation of this platform resulted in 90% of doctors accepting the automatically recommended tests.

The solution is currently used by over 700 healthcare employees from more than 30 medical institutions across the US, with over 1,000 therapy orders created each month. Our team continues to improve the platform, adding new features and integrations with laboratories and insurance companies as more clinics adopt the system.

The implementation of our oncology treatment platform has:

  • Enhanced Evidence-Based Decision Making: Doctors can make accurate, evidence-based treatment decisions by accessing the most relevant studies and automatically generated reports.
  • Streamlined Patient Care: The platform facilitates the analysis of patient cases and the selection of appropriate therapies, improving overall patient care.
  • Increased Efficiency: The automated report generation and seamless integration with existing systems save time and reduce administrative burden, allowing doctors to focus more on patient care.
  • Scalability and Flexibility: The platform is continuously improved and adapted to meet the evolving needs of healthcare providers, ensuring it remains a valuable tool in oncology treatment.

  2. How does integrating automated report generation into an oncology treatment platform benefit healthcare providers?

    Integrating automated report generation allows healthcare providers to quickly access detailed, consistent information needed for making informed treatment decisions. This improves efficiency, saves time, and ensures that doctors have the most relevant data at their fingertips.

  3. What challenges do generic oncology treatment systems pose for healthcare providers?

    Generic oncology treatment systems often fail to provide comprehensive knowledge management, lack automated report generation, and struggle with system integration. These shortcomings can lead to inefficiencies, incomplete patient data, and suboptimal treatment decisions.
