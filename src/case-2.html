About the Client

Our customer is a UK-based agency specializing in clinical and residential care. It operates several care homes and provides mental health rehabilitative services.

Challenge

Addressing the Limitations of Generic PMS Systems in Care Homes: A Custom Solution for Enhanced Patient Care

The customer required us to build a custom patient management system (PMS) for care organizations consisting of several subsystems. The PMS system is needed to manage and analyze medication intake, collect statistics on treatment dynamics per patient, and use the accumulated information to recommend suitable treatments. This would facilitate care providers' decision-making and ensure tailored patient care. Generic PMS systems fall short in all of the following:

  1. Comprehensive Medication Management: Generic PMS systems often fail to provide detailed medication management tailored to individual patient needs. Care homes require a system that can track, analyze, and manage medication intake effectively, ensuring patients receive the correct medication at the right times, without lapsing on med refills.
  2. Dynamic Treatment Analysis: A generic PMS system might not offer the depth of analysis needed to understand patient progress and treatment efficacy, which is vital for mental health rehabilitative services.
  3. Decision Support for Health Care Providers: Care providers need a system that helps them make informed decisions based on real-time data and historical treatment outcomes. A generic PMS system often lacks the integrated decision-support tools necessary for personalized patient care.
  4. Task Management and Automation: Managing tasks within care homes involves numerous repetitive and critical activities. A custom PMS solution should support unlimited automatic tasks with the flexibility to assign them appropriately, reducing administrative burden and ensuring tasks are completed efficiently and on time.
  5. System Integration: The ability to integrate with other parts of the care ecosystem, such as external health systems and APIs, is a necessity for proper care. This ensures seamless data flow and comprehensive care management, which is often lacking in generic PMS solutions.

Given these challenges, the task was to develop a custom PMS that would address the specific needs of care homes, optimize workflows, and provide decision-support tools.

Solution

Custom Cloud-Based PMS for Care Homes: Enhancing Efficiency and Patient Care

Within 10 months, the IT Medical product team developed a cloud-based application allowing the staff at Care Homes to effectively manage their tasks and performance, including monitoring, tracking, analyzing, and reporting. The application also featured an API to enable interaction with other parts of the in-progress ecosystem.

  • Automated Task Management: The system supports an unlimited number of automatic tasks with automated assignee selection. Auto-tasks are preconfigured in the admin panel and activated when triggered inside or by an external system via the API. Tasks can be allocated to multiple employees, with one primary employee in charge, ensuring accountability and efficiency.
  • Medication and Treatment Management: The PMS allows for detailed management and analysis of medication intake, helping to track and adjust treatments as needed. This ensures that each patient’s medication plan is followed accurately, improving treatment outcomes. The system can track side effects, manage refill schedules, improve medication adherence, and identify reasons why a patient might not be taking their medication (e.g., affordability issues, uncomfortable side effects, lack of transportation, insurance coverage gaps, or inability to self-administer medication). This helps reduce polypharmacy and improve overall patient health.
  • Dynamic Reporting and Analytics: The system collects and analyzes data on treatment dynamics per patient, providing valuable insights into the effectiveness of different treatments. This data-driven approach supports care providers in making informed decisions tailored to each patient’s needs.
  • Care Coordination Across Specialties: The system helps physicians from all specialties coordinate care. A significant issue in patient care is the lack of communication between various specialists, which can lead to polypharmacy and adverse health outcomes. For example, a pulmonologist might prescribe a beta-blocker for asthma, while a cardiologist might prescribe a counteracting drug, leading to ineffective treatment. Our PMS addresses this by ensuring that all care providers have access to a patient's comprehensive medication and treatment history, facilitating better coordination and outcomes.
  • Integration Capabilities: The PMS is designed to integrate seamlessly with other healthcare systems through its API. This ensures that all relevant data is accessible and can be utilized effectively across different parts of the care ecosystem.

Outcome

Transforming Care Homes with a Tailored PMS: Improved Efficiency and Patient Outcomes

The IT Medical team developed a PMS as a fully functioning product for Care Home facilities across the UK. Initially planned to be introduced within the agency’s own facilities, the solution was developed to be distributed as a marketable product for other care organizations in the future.

This cloud-based application streamlined the management of tasks related to clinical care while ensuring operational efficiency and transparency. With thousands of predefined tasks and automations, the solution accelerated existing workflows and consequently increased productivity.

FAQ

  1. What are the key benefits of a custom EHR solution compared to a generic system?

    A custom PMS solution is tailored to meet the specific needs of Care Homes, ensuring better alignment with unique workflows. It enhances user experience, integrates seamlessly with other healthcare technologies, and provides decision support tools, ultimately improving efficiency and patient care.

  2. How does integrating decision support tools into a PMS benefit care providers and patients?

    Integrating decision support tools allows care providers to make informed decisions based on real-time data and historical treatment outcomes. This improves patient care by ensuring treatments are tailored to individual needs and are based on comprehensive data analysis.

  3. What challenges do generic PMS systems pose for care homes?

    Generic PMS systems often fail to accommodate the specific workflows of care homes, leading to inefficiencies. They can cause issues with medication management, lack dynamic treatment analysis, offer poor task management, and struggle with system integration and compliance requirements.

  4. How does a custom PMS solution ensure compliance with healthcare regulations?

    A custom PMS solution is designed with built-in compliance features that adhere to healthcare regulations such as HIPAA and local standards. It includes security measures to protect patient data and ensures all regulatory standards are consistently met, reducing the risk of data breaches and legal issues.

  5. Can a custom PMS solution scale with the growth of a care home organization?

    Yes, a custom PMS solution is designed to be scalable and flexible. It can evolve with the growing needs of the care home organization, supporting the addition of new features and integrations with other healthcare services, ensuring continuous improvement and adaptability.
