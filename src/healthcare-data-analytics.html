Benefits of healthcare data analytics

Analytical solutions enable healthcare organizations to use available data to their and patients’ advantage.

  • Patients’ health history analysis can help identify various health risks in advance and prevent upcoming diseases. This allows care providers to avoid hospitalizations, alleviate pressure on the ER and ICU, and speed up patient recovery.
  • A more holistic understanding of all patient health factors helps create treatment plans best suited to each patient’s needs and health specifics. This improves patient satisfaction, boosts treatment success, and makes care more inclusive.
  • Performance analysis helps detect redundant workflows, unproductive resource and personnel usage, and poor management approaches. It also helps establish cost-effective operational strategies, create successful marketing campaigns, and discover opportunities for patient base expansion.
  • Our predictive analytics solutions are particularly effective in crisis management. They can recognize early signs of an upcoming crisis, such as an epidemic, personnel shortages, or facility overcrowding. This allows for practical management approaches, such as redistribution of resources and tasks, data backup, or patient flow redirection, helping your organization stay prepared and proactive.
  • In the pharmaceutical industry, big data analytics and modeling can speed up the processing of research and trial data while also immensely lowering its cost, making new drugs cheaper and safer.

Top Use Cases for Healthcare Data Analytics

  • Disease course prediction

    By analyzing data from EHRs, patient surveys, RPM devices, and nationwide research, healthcare institutions can identify patients at risk of various diseases or predict the worsening of existing conditions.

  • Treatment planning

    By creating a treatment model based on a patient profile with data from various sources, a care provider can better understand the potential effects of different treatments and choose the safest and most effective for each patient.

  • Chronic condition control

    Chronic condition profiling, coupled with continuous patient monitoring and analysis of the readings, offers insights about the condition’s course, alerts patients about potential complications, and provides recommendations.

  • Patient load management

    Modeling patient flow patterns can alert care providers about the influx of patients to particular facilities or specialists, giving them time to prepare and meet the demand without disruptions.

  • Patient engagement

    Learning patients’ behavior patterns can give providers insight into their wants and needs, help better tailor care service offers, and ensure more transparent and satisfying communication with patients.
